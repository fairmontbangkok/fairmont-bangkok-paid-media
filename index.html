SG/AU is a seed presence only. ฿20,325 on "best place to stay in bangkok" gives 4.6% share of voice — meaning your ad shows ~1–2 times per 20 searches. Enough for brand visibility, not for volume.
3
Add negative keywords immediately. Add negatives like "cheap", "budget", "hostel", "agoda", "booking.com" to block irrelevant clicks and protect the budget for high-intent users only.
4
Schedule SG/AU ads for peak booking hours. Limit to 6pm–11pm SG/AU local time (prime travel research window). This concentrates the ฿678/day SG cap on the highest-converting hours.
5
Use ad extensions to boost CTR without extra cost. Add sitelinks (Rooms, Spa, Dining, Offers), callout ("Thailand's first Fairmont"), and location extension. Extensions don't cost extra but increase click rate significantly.
6
Increase to ฿300K to unlock head terms. "Hotel in sukhumvit" (HK/MY ฿52 CPC) and "5 star hotel bangkok" (฿30 CPC) are the next tier. At ฿300K you can add 2–3 more keywords while keeping all current ones fully funded.
